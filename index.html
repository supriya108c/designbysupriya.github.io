My Story

"Design wasn’t something I chose it was something that quietly chose me " I first encountered design not as a career, but as a curiosity. I found myself constantly questioning things others overlooked: Why is this confusing? Why does this feel frustrating? What would make this feel effortless? What began as simple curiosity quickly turned into an obsession with understanding how people think, feel, and interact with the world around them. That’s when I discovered design thinking. It wasn’t just about making things look beautiful it was about solving real problems with empathy at the center. I fell in love with the process: stepping into the user’s perspective, uncovering hidden frustrations, and transforming complexity into clarity. Every insight felt like unlocking a new layer of understanding. Design thinking taught me that great design isn’t what you see it’s what you feel, what you experience, and what you don’t even have to think about. “I didn’t fall in love with design because it looked good I fell in love with it because it made things better.”
